# lit-016 — Apéry (1979), "Irrationalité de ζ(2) et ζ(3)"

**Status:** unverified_paywall_blocked · **Class:** secondary_paper
**Methodological CONTRAST citation only.**

## Citation
Apéry, R. (1979). _Irrationalité de ζ(2) et ζ(3)._ Astérisque **61**, pp. 11–13. (Also a 1978 Marseille lecture in the historical record.)

## Content (standard, abstract-attested)
First proof that ζ(3) is irrational, via an unexpected sequence-recurrence argument with bespoke asymptotic estimates. Single most-cited irrationality result of the 20th century after the classical results on e, π.

## Mission role (methodological contrast)
ζ(3) appears in the proposed C-set for the M1.2 hybrid basis (lit-002 calibration anchor §4). Apéry's result establishes ζ(3) ∈ ℝ \ ℚ but his method is **NOT extensible to K_0**:

- Apéry's proof relies on the specific binomial / hypergeometric identities for ζ(3) sums.
- K_0 has no known analogous binomial / hypergeometric representation.
- This is part of the structural reason the bounded sub-question on K_0 uses PSLQ-null evidence rather than attempting an Apéry-style proof.
