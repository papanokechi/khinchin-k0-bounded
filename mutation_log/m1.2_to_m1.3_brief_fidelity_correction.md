## §3. Generalized binding rule installed at this entry

Per operator directive 2026-05-15 18:45 JST (U-MISSION-G):

> *"Going forward, factual references in the brief (repo names, prior work, machinery claims) are subordinate to empirical verification when verification is cheap. This is a frame correction, not a mission mutation."*

**Rule installed:**

1. When a Brief reference (repo name, prior paper, machinery claim, library availability, version assumption, named-author result) can be empirically verified at <5 minutes of CLI effort, the CLI MUST verify before relying on it.
2. If verification reveals a factual error in the Brief, the CLI:
   - Logs the discrepancy in a `mutation_log/<milestone>_brief_fidelity_correction.md` entry (this entry is the template).
   - Resolves the error by adopting the nearest-empirically-valid substitute and documenting that choice.
   - Does NOT treat this as a mission mutation (Brief §7 budget unaffected).
   - Surfaces the discrepancy to operator in the next reply for ratification (this entry was ratified at 18:45 JST).
3. If verification is *not* cheap (>5 min effort, e.g., reading a 50-page paper to verify a cited theorem), the CLI proceeds under the Brief's claim but flags the unverified dependency in `claims/<id>.json` under `precision_or_dependencies`.

**Generalization of the §3 honest-attribution flag precedent** (2026-05-15 18:28 JST):
The original precedent was operator-side misattribution of a pasted document. The present rule extends that precedent to the *Brief itself* — even the mission's own briefing is subordinate to empirical verification when verification is cheap. This preserves the AEAL audit-trail discipline at the Brief-document layer.
