## 3. Step 2 triggered the halt — H8 paper-read of FBA 1999

`harness/precision_budget.md` §3 documents the H8 paper-read of Ferguson-Bailey-Arno (1999):

- Source: `https://www.davidhbailey.com/dhbpapers/cpslq.pdf` (Bailey archive, canonical preprint of the Math. Comp. 68 (1999) paper)
- SHA-256: `3E330BC11697DBB122D9ADC7357405E7D318DCEE9258CE09BFFD2D47612890B5`
- File: 218,997 bytes, 26 pages; pypdf extraction 51,322 chars saved at `harness/_pslq_candidates/fba1999_text.txt`

What FBA 1999 actually proves (paper-read):
- **Theorem 1**: `M_x ≥ 1/max_i |h_{i,i}(k)|` — per-iteration rigorous certificate
- **Theorem 3**: `|m| ≤ γ^{n-2} M_x` for `γ > 2/√3` (real case)
- **Corollary 2**: PSLQ(τ) terminates in `< (n choose 2)·log_τ(γ^{n-1} M_x)` iterations

What FBA 1999 does NOT contain as a theorem:
- The folklore relation `H ≈ 10^{P/n}` (cited in operator's U-MISSION-K directive as the relation to H8-verify) is **NOT stated as a theorem** in FBA 1999.
- §8 Computer Implementation states descriptively that "Using double precision (i.e., 64-bit) arithmetic, relations of two or three digits in size can be recovered for n up to five or so. Beyond this level, precision is quickly exhausted, and recovered relations and norm bounds are meaningless." — this is observational, not theorem-grade.

**Operator's halt criterion fired:** "If paper-read reveals a different relation, halt and surface."
