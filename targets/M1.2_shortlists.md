## §1 Scoring methodology

Five axes (0–10 each), with the Brief's `P` axis split into `P_pos` and `P_neg` per U-MISSION-E:

| Axis | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **T** | Tractability of the bounded sub-question (clean success/failure boundary under 96h wall-clock and 1000-iter caps from Brief §M3.2) |
| **R** | Reusability of the confirmed machinery base (post-probe canonical set: mpmath 500dps, PSLQ, sympy, gmpy2, numpy, gp shell-out, Lean 4 + Mathlib4, PCF / Painlevé tooling). Entries needing arb/flint/fpylll/sage are penalized. |
| **P_pos** | Publishability of a *positive* / partial result. Acta-class news = 9–10; J Number Theory tier = 6–7; experimental-math note = 3–4. |
| **P_neg** | Publishability of a *negative* / ruling-out result. Structural ruling-out (e.g., escaping residue class) = 6–7; extended FP-rejection range = 5–7; "no counterexample in range" without structural insight = 2–4. |
| **I** | Independence from R1 / Painlevé III queue. 9 = zero overlap; 6–7 = machinery overlap only; ≤ 5 = content overlap. |

Composites:

- `C_primary  = T + R + P_pos + I` (max 40) → drives primary shortlist
- `C_contrast = T + R + P_neg + I` (max 40) → drives contrast shortlist

Deterministic tie-break ladder: composite desc → relevant P axis desc → T desc → problem-name alphabetical.

AEAL hygiene note: scores are **judgment-class evidence**, not numerical-class. The harness is reproducible (`python build_m1_2_shortlists.py` emits identical JSON), but the score values themselves are a commit to a specific reading of the literature on the M1.2 date. No `claims/` entry is produced by this script.
